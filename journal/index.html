Overlays Design

April 23, 2026

When using overlays it is important to keep in mind to use them sparingly. While they can free up a lot of space on the user's screen, they can be very annoying for users. Some best practices include, using them for smaller interactive instances such as attention grabbers for users, sign in/ log in information from users, showing extra larger images or videos within the context of the main page, and when showing information that isn’t in a different context than the main page. They should consist of a title, some way to exit out of it, a button, a focus effect, and shouldn’t take up more than 50% of the screen, with it being placed towards the top of your page.

Research Form Design

April 14, 2026

In Salim Ansari’s “Best practices for form design” article he talks about what are important things you should keep in mind when it comes to designing a form. One of the most important ones being that you DO NOT make them too long. Users do not want to have to fill out way too many questions. You should really focus on only asking the most important and relevant questions. In the world of form design less is definitely more. He also mentions that it's good to keep it down to a one column layout because it's much easier for people to view and navigate, then if it were to be multiple. Another thing that was mentioned is that if you give users a clear way to see their progress through the form, and how much is left for them to fill out, they are more likely to finish the form then if they go in blind, and don’t have any way of knowing how much is left.

I think the fact that they numbered each point on the website, and added hierarchy levels, makes it so that people can digest the information, and get a good understanding of each of the points without feeling obligated to spend a lot of time dissecting the website.
